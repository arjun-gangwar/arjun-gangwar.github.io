In the [previous post](/blog/flow-matching-notes) we looked at the
conditional flow matching objective. The core idea is easier to *see* than
to read: under the optimal-transport path, every sample just travels in a
straight line from the prior to the data distribution[^1].

## The trajectories

Drag the slider to move time $t$ from the prior ($t=0$) to the target
distribution ($t=1$):

<FlowTrajectories />

Each dot follows $x_t = (1-t)\,x_0 + t\,x_1$, so the target vector field is
constant along the path: $u_t(x \mid x_1) = x_1 - x_0$. That constancy is
exactly why so few ODE steps suffice at inference time[^2].
